
## The Code Legal of [[Thraldur]]

#### Declared by [[King Aldurin Thamar]] of [[Thraldur]]

~**The use of magic in any form, is illegal and considered as an act of Treason. Those caught performing acts of magic, in the possession of a magic item, or attempting to produce or procure a magic item, may suffer any, or all, of those consequences listed: flogging, imprisonment, exile, or death**  
~**The worship of a divine being or deity of any kind is considered as Treason**  
~**Possession of a weapon of any sort without being authorized to own a weapon is considered as an act of Treason and the offenders shall be at the mercy of the King**

#### I. Crimes against Lords, Officials, and Nobles

~**Assaulting or impersonating a Royal or Lord: death**  
~**Assaulting or impersonating an official or noble: flogging, imprisonment up to a fortnight, and fine up to 500gp**  
~**Blackmailing an official: flogging and exile up to 10 years**  
~**Bribery or attempted bribery of an official: exile up to 20 years and fine up to double the bribe amount**  
~**Murder of a Royal, Lord, official, or noble: death**

#### II. Crimes against the City

~**Arson: death or hard labour up to 1 year, with fines and/or damages covering the cost of the repairs plus 2,000 gp**  
~**Brandishing weapons without due cause: imprisonment up to a fortnight and/or fine up to 10 gp**  
~**Espionage: death or permanent exile**  
~**Fencing stolen goods: fine equal to the value of the stolen goods and edict**  
~**Forgery of an official document: flogging and exile for 10 summers**  
~**Hampering justice: fine up to 200 gp and hard labour up to a fortnight**  
~**Littering: fine up to 2 gp and edict**  
~**Poisoning a city well: death**  
~**Theft: flogging followed by imprisonment up to a fortnight, hard labour up to 1 year, or fine equal to the value of the stolen goods**  
~**Treason: death**  
~**Vandalism: imprisonment up to a fortnight plus fine and/or damages covering the cost of repairs plus up to 100 gp**

#### III. Crimes against Citizens

~**Assaulting a citizen: imprisonment up to a fortnight, flogging, and damages up to 1,000 gp**  
~**Blackmailing or intimidating a citizen: fine or damages up to 500 gp and edict**  
~**Burglary: imprisonment up to 3 months and damages equal to the value of the stolen goods plus 500 gp** ~**Damaging property or livestock: damages covering the cost of repairs or replacement plus up to 500 gp** ~**Desecration of a corpse: death or hard labour up to 10 years, and damages up to 1,000 gp paid to the kin** ~**Disturbing the peace: fine up to 25 gp and edict** ~**Murdering a citizen without justification: death or hard labour up to 10 years, and damages up to 1,000 gp paid to the victim's kin**  
~**Murdering a citizen with justification: exile up to 5 years or hard labour up to 3 years or damages up to 1,000 gp paid to the victim's kin**  
~**Robbery: hard labour up to 1 month and damages equal to the value of the stolen goods plus 500 gp**  
~**Slavery: flogging and hard labour up to 10 years**
